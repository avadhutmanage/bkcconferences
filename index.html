About the College
Established 1975 · Belgaum, Karnataka

D.M.S. Mandal's Bhaurao Kakatkar College has been imparting quality education to students of the border district of Belgaum since 1975. Named after philanthropist Late Shri. Bhaurao Dhanajirao Kakatkar, the college promotes scientific temper, self-reliance, and self-esteem among unprivileged young people.

The college offers aided UG Courses in Commerce, Arts, Science and self-finance courses including M.Com and M.Sc. Chemistry. Re-accredited at 'A' Grade by NAAC with CGPA 3.11.

About the Conference

The International Conference on Advances in Physical, Chemical and Mathematical Sciences for Sustainable Development (ICAPCMSSD 2026) is jointly organized by the Department of Physics, Department of Chemistry, Department of Mathematics and IQAC of Bhaurao Kakatkar College, Belgaum.

The conference aims to bring together researchers, academicians, industry experts, and students from across the world to share recent advances and innovative ideas in physics, chemistry, and mathematics — with a focus on interdisciplinary research contributing to sustainable development and addressing global challenges such as energy sustainability, environmental protection, advanced materials, and technological innovation.

Objectives

  • Promote interdisciplinary research in physical, chemical, and mathematical sciences
  • Discuss recent developments and innovations contributing to sustainable development
  • Provide a platform for researchers, academicians, and students to share their work
  • Encourage collaboration between academia, research institutions, and industry
  • Explore scientific solutions for global environmental and technological challenges

About the Seminar

The seminar "Social Sciences in the Age of AI: History, Policy, and Society" addresses the profound, multi-dimensional impact of Artificial Intelligence on human structures, research methodologies, and governance. It explores how AI is reshaping social science disciplines such as history, sociology, economics, and political science.

As AI transitions from a technical tool to a social force, it is redefining the human experience. This seminar provides an interdisciplinary platform to analyze how AI impacts historical research, public policy frameworks, and the very structure of society — moving beyond 'tech-hype' to understand the ethical, legal, and sociological realities of the 21st century.

About the Seminar

In a world grappling with complex challenges, the Indian Knowledge System (IKS) offers profound, holistic insights. This vast repository of India's intellectual, scientific, and philosophical heritage provides sustainable paradigms relevant to contemporary issues — from environmental concerns to ethical technology and holistic well-being.

This seminar, jointly organized by the Departments of Marathi, English, Kannada, and Hindi, serves as a vital interdisciplinary platform to explore how ancient Indian wisdom can be effectively integrated with modern advancements. Scholars, practitioners, and innovators are invited to rediscover and re-contextualize IKS for global betterment.
